As the financial impact of first-party fraud continues to rise, Credit Abuse Risk was developed to uncover
atypical patterns indicative of two types of fraudulent activities: loan stacking, when an individual quickly
applies for multiple loans with no intent to repay those loans, and credit washing, when a person tries to
remove accurate, but negative information from a credit report. These patterns can be identified during
prequalification offers, account origination, or portfolio review. This allows lenders to modify loan terms

"By focusing on application behavior in real-time, Credit Abuse Risk quickly helps to reduce the potential
for fraud and related costs," said Felipe Castillo, Chief Product Officer for U.S. Information Solutions at
Equifax. "This supports a more confident lending environment, and helps keep credit available for
consumers."
