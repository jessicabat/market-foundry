Earnings Call AnalysisQ4-2023 AnalysisAmerican Express CoAmerican Express Touts Strong 2023 PerformanceAmerican Express celebrated a victorious 2023, with a remarkable $61 billion in annual revenue, climbing 15% from the previous year, and a net income passing $8 billion. Their success story includes a fourth-quarter revenue hitting nearly $16 billion, yielding an EPS of $2.62. Over two years, the company broadened its scale by over 40%, attracting around 25 million new cardholders. In acknowledging the year's success, the company projects a solid future with an estimated revenue growth of 9-11% and an EPS forecast of $12.65 to $13.15 for 2024. Moreover, investors can look forward to a bountiful dividend growth over 15% to $0.70 per quarter as the company continues its commitment to reward shareholders.Consumer Billings Growth and Spending TrendsIn a consumer-centric performance, the U.S. Consumer segment grew billings by 7% and demonstrated a notable increase in spending, with a 15% rise, propelled by millennials and Gen Z customers who led the charge in higher bill business growth within this segment.Small and Medium-Sized Enterprise (SME) Segment SteadinessGrowth in the SME segment remained at a consistent 1%. Despite modest spending growth attributed to prior inventory restocking challenges, there was a silver lining with strong demand for new accounts, sustained high retention rates, and robust credit performance on small business products.International Market ExpansionOn an international scale, consumers, SMEs, and large corporate customers each saw a 13% upturn in the previous quarter. Though the economic climate is subdued, there is satisfaction with the steadfast loyalty and engagement of card members globally.Revenue Increases and ProjectionsThe company's total revenue rose by an impressive 11% year-over-year in the fourth quarter, with a full year increase of 15% on an FX-adjusted basis. Looking ahead, the company's focus on premium product offerings and planned refreshes of approximately 40 products globally underpin expectations of continued revenue momentum, projecting growth between 9% to 11% in 2024.Interest Income and Expense ManagementNet interest income skyrocketed by 30% year-over-year in Q4, driven by an uptick in revolving loan balances and yield expansion. While growth rates are expected to moderate as balance growth slows, a strong card member base should sustain positive trends. Expense growth, having been kept significantly lower than revenue growth, manifested a strategic focus on driving operating leverage with a prediction of moderate increase through the upcoming year.Credit Quality and ReservesDespite a moderate uptick in write-offs and delinquencies, strong and best-in-class credit metrics are still apparent. This is attributed to the high credit quality of the customer base and rigorous risk management strategies, with a slight projected increase in the reserve rate for 2024. Total provisions for the fourth quarter reached $1.4 billion.Capital Return to ShareholdersShareholders benefited from a return of $5.3 billion in capital through 2023, signaling solid earnings generation. The company maintains a comfortable CET1 ratio and has foreshadowed a dividend increase by over 15% for Q1 2024, demonstrating continued commitment to shareholder returns without significant deviations from its existing capital management strategy.Travel & Entertainment (T&E) Spend and Credit PerformanceWhile T&E growth rates have decelerated, there is still a substantial year-over-year increase of 9%. Bookings remain robust, and there's no immediate concern over an emerging downward trend.
